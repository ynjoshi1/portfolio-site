Formula SAE Suspension System

Project Overview

Designed a high-performance double-wishbone suspension system for MIT's Formula SAE vehicle. The project encompassed complete lifecycle development from initial concept through fabrication and track validation.

Technical Highlights

  • Kinematic Optimization: MATLAB simulation achieving 0.5¬∞ camber change through 3" of travel
  • Structural Validation: FEA analysis showing max von Mises stress of 45 ksi under 3g loading (SF=2.2)
  • Weight Reduction: 18% lighter than previous design through topology optimization and material selection
  • Manufacturing: Produced fabrication drawings with complete GD&T callouts per ASME Y14.5

Results

The suspension system contributed to a 2nd place finish at the 2024 Formula SAE Michigan competition. Improved handling metrics and reduced lap times by 1.8 seconds on the autocross course.

Multi-Axis CNC Enclosure

Project Overview

Custom aluminum enclosure designed for a high-precision sensor assembly. The project emphasized Design for Manufacturing principles to optimize both cost and quality.

Technical Highlights

  • DFM Optimization: Redesigned features to eliminate unnecessary setups, reducing machining time by 35%
  • Tolerance Analysis: Statistical tolerance stack-up ensuring critical dimensions held ¬±0.001"
  • Toolpath Efficiency: Generated optimized CAM toolpaths in Mastercam with strategic use of high-speed machining strategies
  • Quality Control: Performed CMM inspection on all critical features, achieving Cpk > 1.67

Results

Successfully manufactured 15 units with zero defects. Process improvements documented in technical report and adopted as standard practice for future enclosure designs.

Pressure Vessel Optimization

Project Overview

Nonlinear finite element analysis of a composite pressure vessel subjected to cyclic internal pressure. Objective was to reduce weight while maintaining structural integrity per ASME Section VIII standards.

Technical Highlights

  • Material Model: Implemented progressive damage models for carbon fiber/epoxy laminate
  • Loading Conditions: Applied 0-200 psi cyclic pressure with fatigue life prediction using S-N curves
  • Mesh Convergence: Refined mesh to 250K elements, achieving <2% stress variation on sequential refinements
  • Optimization: Python scripting automated 50+ design iterations, converging on optimal ply layup

Results

Final design achieved 22% weight reduction compared to baseline aluminum vessel. All stress concentrations remained below material allowables with minimum safety factor of 2.5. Results validated through burst testing at external lab.
